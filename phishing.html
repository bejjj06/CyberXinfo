Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks are deceptive attempts to steal sensitive information by masquerading as trustworthy entities. These attacks combine technical deception with social engineering to trick victims into revealing personal data.

Overview

Phishing remains one of the most effective attack vectors because it exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. By creating a sense of urgency, fear, or curiosity, attackers can bypass security systems and trick even security-conscious individuals.

Email Phishing

The most common form involves sending emails that appear to come from legitimate organizations, requesting verification of information or containing malicious attachments.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks directed at specific individuals or companies, customized with personal information to increase credibility and success rates.

Smishing

Phishing conducted via SMS text messages, often containing links to malicious websites or requesting immediate action.

Vishing

Voice phishing uses phone calls to deceive victims into revealing personal information or making payments to scammers.

How They Work

A typical phishing attack follows these steps:

• Planning - Attackers identify targets and create convincing fake content
• Delivery - The phishing message is sent via email, text, social media, etc.
• Deception - The message creates urgency or fear to prompt immediate action
• Collection - Victims enter credentials or personal data on fake websites
• Exploitation - Attackers use stolen information for fraud or further attacks

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify the sender - Check email addresses carefully for misspellings or unusual domains
  • Be suspicious of urgency - Legitimate organizations rarely demand immediate action
  • Hover before clicking - Check link destinations before clicking by hovering over them
  • Use direct sources - Type URLs directly or use bookmarks instead of clicking email links
  • Enable 2FA - Two-factor authentication provides an extra layer of security
  • Use password managers - These won't auto-fill credentials on fake websites
  • Keep software updated - Updates often include security patches against known phishing techniques
