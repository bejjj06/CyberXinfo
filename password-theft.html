Password Theft & Account Access

One of the most common goals for attackers is stealing login credentials (usernames and passwords) to gain unauthorized access to accounts, systems, and sensitive data.

Overview

Stolen credentials can be used for identity theft, financial fraud, accessing private communications, or as a stepping stone to compromise organizational networks. Protecting your passwords is a fundamental aspect of cybersecurity.

Brute Force Attacks

Attackers systematically try all possible password combinations until the correct one is found. Often targets weak or common passwords.

Credential Stuffing

Using lists of stolen usernames and passwords (from data breaches) to try logging into many different websites, hoping users reused credentials.

Keyloggers (Malware)

Malware that secretly records every keystroke you make, including usernames and passwords, and sends them to the attacker.

Phishing & Social Engineering

Tricking users into voluntarily revealing their credentials on fake login pages or through deceptive emails, calls, or messages.

How Passwords Are Stolen

Attackers employ various tactics to acquire login details:

• Data Breaches - Leaked databases from compromised websites
• Phishing Campaigns - Deceptive emails/websites tricking users
• Malware Infections - Keyloggers, info-stealers on victim devices
• Password Spraying - Trying common passwords against many accounts
• Shoulder Surfing - Physically watching someone enter credentials
• Exploiting Weaknesses - Using default or easily guessable passwords

How to Protect Your Accounts

  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords - Combine upper/lowercase letters, numbers, symbols. Different for every site.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA/2FA) - Adds a crucial extra layer of security beyond just the password.
  • Use a Password Manager - Securely generates, stores, and fills complex passwords.
  • Beware of Phishing - Scrutinize emails/messages asking for login info or directing to login pages.
  • Keep Software Updated - Protects against malware that could steal credentials.
  • Monitor Account Activity - Check for unrecognized logins or changes.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Logins - Use a VPN if you must log in on public networks.
